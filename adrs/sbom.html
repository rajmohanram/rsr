A Software Bill of Materials (SBOM) is a detailed inventory that lists all the components, libraries, and dependencies included in a software product, along with their versions and licenses. It helps organizations ensure transparency, security, and compliance by providing visibility into open-source and third-party software used in applications. In the context of Red Hat Enterprise Linux (RHEL), SBOMs are increasingly important for tracking the security and compliance of system components, as RHEL integrates numerous open-source packages. This enables RHEL users to quickly identify vulnerabilities, manage risks, and meet regulatory requirements by auditing and monitoring software components.
